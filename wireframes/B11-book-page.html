2 — What's inside

What's inside

The Okay to Ask book collects real, anonymous questions from young people — written on post-it notes during RSE sessions in schools — and answers every one of them with honesty and care.

Inside you'll find post-it scans alongside clear, age-appropriate answers covering relationships, sex, bodies, identity, safety, and the law. It's the physical version of everything on this site — a book you can pick up, put down, and come back to.

This section sells the format: post-its + answers. It bridges the gap between the site and the book — visitors who've been reading answers online now understand what the book adds (physicality, browsability, a thing you can hand to someone).
3 — Who it's for

Who it's for

Parents and carers

A conversation starter for home. Leave it on the coffee table, the kitchen counter, their bedside table. It opens doors to conversations that might not happen otherwise.

School staff rooms

A reference that's always on the shelf. Teachers can dip into it before lessons, or leave it where students can browse it themselves.

Conferences and training

A credibility artefact. The book demonstrates what young people actually want to know — grounding any presentation or CPD session in their real questions.

Young people

A book that treats them with respect. No condescension, no scare tactics — just honest answers to the things they're actually wondering.

Four audience cards. This section is important because the book serves genuinely different use cases. Each card speaks directly to that audience — "you" language, not third person.

Annotations
→ High-traffic destination: Every question page's end-of-answer panel (A17) links here via the "young people / parents" CTA. With 152 question pages, this is one of the most-linked-to pages on the site. It needs to convert — the content should be compelling, not just informational.
→ Okay to Ask zone: This page belongs to the Okay to Ask visual identity — warm, post-it textures, young-people-facing tone. It should feel like the OTA landing (B8), not the B2B services pages.
→ "Who it's for" does real work: This isn't filler — the book genuinely serves different audiences with different motivations. A parent browsing at home needs a different pitch than a teacher stocking a staff room. The audience cards let each visitor see themselves in the product.
→ Purchase CTA flexibility: Three variants wireframed because the sales approach is TBD. The developer builds all three and Gareth activates the right one via CMS or config. "Register interest" is the safest launch-day default.
→ Sample content links back to site: The "Read full answer" links on sample questions create a loop: site → book page → sample → back to site. This keeps visitors engaged even if they don't buy — the book page isn't a dead end.
→ Bulk pricing mention: "Schools: bulk pricing available" in the purchase CTA is a lightweight B2B nudge without breaking the consumer-facing tone. Teachers seeing this page will note it; parents will skim past it.

Design decisions flagged

Book cover image: A high-quality photo or render of the physical book is essential — this is a product page. If the cover isn't finalised, a styled placeholder showing the Okay to Ask branding works at launch, but a real image should replace it as soon as possible.

Purchase CTA position: Currently section 4 of 6 — after "who it's for" but before samples and trust. This follows a persuasion arc: what it is → who it's for → buy it → proof it's good. An alternative is to place the CTA after the samples (section 5), so the visitor sees examples before being asked to buy. Either works — test and iterate.

Sticky buy button: On mobile, a sticky "Buy now" bar at the bottom of the screen could improve conversion. The visitor can scroll through all the content and tap buy at any point without scrolling back up. Design decision.

Post-it image quality: The sample content section should use actual post-it scans (photos of real handwritten questions), not illustrated recreations. The authenticity of real handwriting is a huge part of the book's appeal.

Mobile: Book cover scales down but stays prominent. Audience cards stack. Purchase block is full-width. Sample questions stack with post-it thumbnails to the left.
