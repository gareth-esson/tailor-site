Annotations
1 Dual hero: Sections 1 and 2 are the fork. A teacher scanning the page sees the professional hero first and can act immediately. A young person sees the Okay to Ask section immediately below and is drawn in by the visual shift. The two shouldn't compete — there should be a clear tonal and visual break between them.
2 Visual identity shift: Section 2 marks the transition into Okay to Ask territory — warmer colours, post-it motif, informal tone. This shift is previewed here and fully realised on B8 and C1.
3 Book promotion (Section 3): Sits within the Okay to Ask visual identity zone (warm background like the OTA hero). Book cover placeholder, one-line pitch, CTA to B11. Hero-level prominence, not a sidebar.
4 "What Tailor does" (Section 4): Brief and scannable. Not a mission statement — three concrete things with links. A school leader who skipped the hero can orient here.
5 Featured questions (Section 5): This is the Okay to Ask taster that draws young people deeper. Post-it thumbnails make these visually distinct from the professional content above. Selection is either curated or flagged in the DB.
6 Trust signals (Section 6): Framework badges + a testimonial pull quote. This is for the school leader or parent who needs reassurance. Link to /our-approach for the full story.
7 Topics overview (Section 7): Serves both audiences — a teacher can browse by curriculum area, a young person can explore by interest. Shows 7 categories grouped by the app, with 22 landing pages total across all categories.

Design decisions flagged

Hero split: Sections 1 and 2 could be a true 50/50 split (side by side on desktop), or sequential with a clear visual break. The wireframe shows them stacked (content hierarchy only) — art direction will determine the actual layout relationship.

Featured questions source: Curated (Gareth picks 3–5 questions manually) or auto-populated (most viewed, or a "featured" boolean in the Questions DB). Curated is better for launch; auto-populated scales better.

Mobile: All sections stack naturally. The dual hero becomes two sequential blocks. Featured questions may become a horizontal scroll.

Seventh category? The topic counts are placeholder — actual category count and distribution will come from the Topics DB. The grid should handle 6–8 categories gracefully.
