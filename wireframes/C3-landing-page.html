Annotations
C3 Aggregation model: Each landing page maps to multiple granular app topics. "Consent" the landing page might aggregate "consent", "consent and the law", "consent in relationships" from the app's 83-topic taxonomy. Questions, glossary terms, and blog posts are pulled from all the granular topics this page covers.
A11 Explore lessons CTA: Mid-page. Links to the app library filtered by this landing page's topic slug. The primary conversion for teachers looking for resources.
A12 Bring to school CTA: Bottom of page. Target varies per topic — some topics naturally map to delivery, others to training or consultancy. The "Service CTA Target" field on the Topics DB drives this.
A15 Related content cards: Three variants used here — question cards (Section 6), glossary term list (Section 7), and blog post list (Section 8). Same A15 component family, different content types.
→ No Simple Mode: Landing pages don't have a Standard/Simple toggle. They're teacher-facing reference pages. Simple Mode is for question pages (C1) and glossary pages (C2) only.
→ Dual audience: Despite being teacher-facing, the questions list (Section 6) serves young people too. A student who lands here from a topic tag sees questions they can click into. The page doesn't exclude them — it just leads with the teacher lens.

Design decisions flagged

Content depth: The overview (Section 2) and curriculum mapping (Section 3) are the teacher-trust content. They need to be thorough enough to be useful but not so long they push the questions and CTAs below the fold. The overview should be 2–3 paragraphs max.

Questions list length: Some landing pages may aggregate dozens of questions. Consider a "Show more" toggle after 5–8, or a horizontal scroll on mobile. The full list shouldn't overwhelm the page.

Empty states: Some landing pages may launch without blog posts (Section 8) or with very few questions. The template needs to handle these gracefully — conditional sections that simply don't render when empty.

Category label: The category shown next to the topic name (Section 1) groups this page within the 7 app categories. Whether the label is clickable (filtering /topics/ by category) or purely informational is a design decision.

22 pages, one template: All 22 landing pages use this exact template. The content varies (topic name, overview, curriculum refs, questions, terms, blog posts, CTA targets) but the structure is identical. Build once, populate 22 times.
