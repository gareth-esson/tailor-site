Annotations
→ Four services, one hub: Training (B4) is a standalone page. The other three (delivery, drop-days, consultancy) use the C5 template. All four are linked from this hub. Training gets slightly more prominence because it's the highest-traffic path.
→ Card order: Training first (highest traffic, app connection). Then delivery (the "do it for us" option — likely the most intuitive service). Then drop-days (a variant of delivery). Then consultancy (the "help us do it ourselves" option). This order goes from most to least hands-on.
→ SEO role: This page is the landing page for broad service queries ("RSE services for schools", "RSE provider", "RSE training and delivery"). The individual pages (B4, C5) handle more specific queries ("RSE CPD training", "RSE consultancy for schools").
→ Lightweight by design: This page is deliberately brief. It's a routing page — the job is to help the visitor find the right service page, not to sell them on this page. The depth lives on B4 and the three C5 pages.

Design decisions flagged

Training highlight: The wireframe suggests a visual accent on the training card (thicker border, warm background) since it's the highest-traffic service and the only one with the app connection. This could be too heavy-handed if the other services are equally important commercially — Gareth's call.

Card layout: Shown stacked (single column) for hierarchy clarity. On desktop, these could be a 2×2 grid. Stacked is better for scanning; grid is better for showing them as equals. Design decision.

Icons: Placeholder letters shown. Real icons or illustrations would help differentiate the services visually. Even simple abstract icons (a person teaching, a calendar, a conversation) would improve scanability.

Price indicators: Not included. If Gareth wants to add "from £X" to any cards, it would sit after the description. This is a commercial decision — some providers prefer to lead with value, others with transparency.
