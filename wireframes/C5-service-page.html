2 — What the service is

Clear description of what happens when a school books this service. What does Gareth actually deliver? What does the day look like? What does the school get out of it?

This isn't a features list — it's a narrative explanation. A school leader reading this should be able to picture what the engagement looks like from start to finish.

3 — Who it's for

For schools who want high-quality RSE delivered directly to students by a specialist — either because they don't have a confident PSHE lead, because they want expert input on sensitive topics, or because they need a one-off session for a specific year group.

The buyer persona in plain language. Helps the reader self-select — "this is for me" or "I need something else." Different for each of the 3 service pages.
4 — What's included

What's included

Pre-visit planning call to agree topics, year groups, and any sensitivities
Direct delivery to students — Key Stage 2, 3, or 4
Duration and group size agreed per school
Post-session summary and follow-up resources for staff
Disclosure-aware delivery — trained to handle disclosures sensitively and in line with school safeguarding procedures
Specifics: key stages covered, duration, what Gareth delivers, what the school provides. Concrete enough that a school leader can assess fit without needing to call first.

The three instances — content differences
/delivery Direct RSE Delivery — Gareth comes into the school and delivers lessons directly to students. The "we do it for you" option. Persona: schools without a confident PSHE lead, or wanting expert input on sensitive topics.
/drop-days Drop Day Delivery — A full-day or half-day intensive RSE programme for a year group. The "event" option. Persona: schools wanting a focused, impactful day rather than drip-fed lessons.
/consultancy RSE Consultancy — Gareth advises the school on their RSE programme, policy, and curriculum. The "help us do it ourselves" option. Persona: schools with some capacity but wanting expert guidance on structure, content, and compliance.

Design decisions flagged

CTA placement: The enquiry CTA (Section 5) is positioned mid-page — after "what's included" but before the testimonial and related content. This gives the reader enough information to act without scrolling to the bottom. Consider repeating the CTA at the very end of the page as well, for visitors who read everything first.

Pricing: The brief doesn't mention pricing. If Gareth wants to show indicative pricing ("from £X per day"), it would slot naturally into Section 4 (what's included) or as a standalone section between 4 and 5. If pricing is bespoke per school, the current structure works — the CTA drives to a conversation, not a checkout.

"What's included" format: Shown here as a list with icons. Could also be a grid of feature cards or a simple prose description. The list format is scannable and helps school leaders quickly assess what they're getting.

Related topics scope: How many topics to show? 3–5 is enough to demonstrate breadth without overwhelming. The selection should be the most commonly requested topics for this service type.
