## 6. Spot illustrations — Okay to Ask landing (B8)

| Illustration | Section | Description |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **Anatomy** | Category browsing | A simplified, respectful human body outline or torso — not a medical diagram, more like a warm line drawing of a figure with a gentle highlight on the body. Could also be an abstract arrangement of body-related shapes (organs, cells) as a pattern. |
| **Puberty** | Category browsing | A figure mid-change — growing, stretching, becoming. **Option A:** A caterpillar/butterfly metaphor (might feel too young). **Option B:** A single figure at different heights, suggesting growth over time. **Option C:** A mirror with a reflection that's slightly different — you're changing and that's okay. |
| **Relationships** | Category browsing | Two or more figures connected. **Option A:** Two people side by side, arms linked or overlapping — warmth, closeness. **Option B:** A web or constellation of small figures connected by lines — the breadth of relationships (friends, family, romantic). Not just a couple. |
| **Sex** | Category browsing | The hardest one to get right. Must be honest without being graphic or coy. **Option A:** Two abstract figures close together, heads touching or overlapping — intimacy without anatomy. **Option B:** An unmade bed — universally understood, gentle, not explicit. **Option C:** Two hands interlocking — connection, trust, choice. |
| **Sexual Health** | Category browsing | Clinical enough to mean health, warm enough to not feel scary. **Option A:** A stethoscope or medical cross combined with a heart or body shape. **Option B:** A clinic door that's open — "this is accessible, not intimidating." **Option C:** A condom, a testing kit, and a checkmark together — practical, normalised. |
| **Sex & the Law** | Category browsing | Legal framework without feeling threatening. **Option A:** A scales-of-justice motif, simplified and warm — not courtroom-serious. **Option B:** A document or scroll with a tick/check — law as protection, not punishment. **Option C:** A shield — law exists to keep you safe. |
| **Contraception & Pregnancy** | Category browsing | Practical and normalised. **Option A:** A contraceptive pill packet, a condom, and a pregnancy test as a cluster — the real objects, illustrated warmly. **Option B:** A timeline or path with branch points — representing choices. The feeling should be "you have options and information." |

7 illustrations. These make the browse-by-category section feel rich and inviting rather than just coloured text labels. They should feel consistent as a set — same style, weight, and level of abstraction. The tone across all 7 should be "warm, honest, unafraid" — matching the Okay to Ask voice.

## 12. Spot illustrations — Signposting and support (A8, A9)

| Illustration | Component | Description |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **"Need support?"** | Signposting block (A8) | Appears on every question page. Must draw the eye to help without creating anxiety. **Option A:** An outstretched hand — open, offering, not grabbing. **Option B:** A speech bubble with a heart inside — "someone is here to listen." **Option C:** A phone or chat icon with a warm glow around it — "help is one call away." Should feel warm, visible, and kind. Not clinical (no red crosses), not childish (no cartoon characters), not alarming (no warning triangles). The visual language should say "this is here if you need it" rather than "something is wrong." |
| **"Need help right now?"** | Crisis support (A9) | Appears above the answer on safeguarding-related questions. More urgent than A8 but still not frightening. **Option A:** The same outstretched hand as A8 but larger and more prominent — same visual language, elevated urgency. **Option B:** A figure with arms open — "come here, it's safe." **Option C:** A lifeline or rope — "hold on to this." Must feel serious and immediate without being frightening. A young person reading about abuse who IS being abused needs to see this and feel "that's for me" — not "that looks scary." The art direction brief says these must never feel decorative. |

2 illustrations. These are the most important illustrations on the site from a safeguarding perspective. Get these right and they could genuinely help someone. The visual tone should be consistent with each other — A9 is a louder version of A8, not a different visual language.

## 13. Spot illustrations — Empty states and errors

| Illustration | Context | Description |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| **404 page** | Page not found | Brand personality moment — the one page where you can be playful. **Option A:** A post-it note that's been blown away by the wind, with a "?" on it — the question got lost. **Option B:** A figure looking at a map with a confused expression — "you've wandered off the path." **Option C:** An empty question box with a cobweb — "nothing here." Should feel friendly and redirecting ("here's how to get back"), not broken or corporate ("error 404"). |
| **No search results** | Search results (B10) | The reader searched for something and nothing matched. **Option A:** A magnifying glass with nothing under it — "we looked, but..." **Option B:** An empty post-it note — the question hasn't been asked yet. **Option C:** A figure shrugging gently with a half-smile — "not yet, but try something else." Should feel helpful and encouraging, not discouraging. |
| **No blog posts yet** | Landing page conditional section | A landing page section where blog posts would normally appear, but none exist yet for this topic. **Option A:** A pencil resting on a blank page — "we're writing this." **Option B:** A "coming soon" seed planted in soil — growth is happening. Keep it very small and subtle — this is a conditional empty state, not a feature. |

3 illustrations.

**Naming conventions:**
- Files named by slug (matching the URL or database slug field)
- Post-its named by question slug (matching `/anonymous_question/{slug}`)

- All photos as `.webp` with fallback `.png` if needed
- All spot illustrations and logos as `.svg`
- OG images as `.png` (social platforms handle PNG most reliably)
