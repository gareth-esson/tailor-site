Section 5 — What is Okay to Ask?

Where do these questions come from?

Every question on this site was written anonymously by a real young person on a post-it note during an RSE lesson. They were answered by Gareth Esson, a specialist RSE consultant who has worked with thousands of young people in schools across the UK.

No question is too awkward, too small, or too weird. If a young person asked it, it's okay to ask.

Annotations
1 Visual identity: This entire page lives in Okay to Ask territory — warm colours, post-it motif, informal voice. It should feel different from the B2B showcase pages. The header (A1) is structurally the same but may carry warmer visual treatment via CSS tokens.
2 Book promotion (Section 2): Hero-level element within the Okay to Ask visual identity. Book cover placeholder, one-line pitch, purchase CTA to B11. Same warm OTA identity as the hero.
3 Topic filtering (Section 3): 6 Okay to Ask book categories (Anatomy, Puberty, Relationships, Sex, Sexual Health, Sex and the Law). These are distinct from the app's 7 categories and the site's 22 landing pages. Selecting a category filters the question list below without a page reload. "All questions" is the default.
4 Question list (Section 4): Each item shows the post-it thumbnail, question title as a link, and a topic tag. The post-it thumbnail is the visual hook — it makes this feel like real questions, not a database dump. Links go to /anonymous_question/{slug} (C1).
5 "What is Okay to Ask?" (Section 5): This grounds the project for anyone who's arrived directly. Brief — not a manifesto. Establishes that these are real questions from real young people, answered by a named expert.
6 Signposting (Section 6): A general "Need support?" block — not contextual to any specific question. Safety net for anyone browsing who might be going through something. Key services only (Childline, The Mix, Brook). This is distinct from the question-specific signposting (A8) on individual question pages.

Design decisions flagged

Default sort order: Should 152 questions appear by topic, by popularity, randomly shuffled, or newest first? Alphabetical by question title would be unhelpful. By topic (grouped) or by popularity (most viewed) are the strongest options.

Pagination vs infinite scroll: With 152 questions, showing all at once is unwieldy. Options: paginated (20 per page), "load more" button, or infinite scroll. "Load more" is the gentlest — it doesn't lose the user's scroll position and doesn't overwhelm.

Post-it thumbnails: These are the scanned handwritten question images. On this listing page they appear as small thumbnails. They're key to the identity — without them this is just a list of links.

Mobile: Topic filter chips may horizontally scroll. Question list items stack naturally. Post-it thumbnails could shrink or be hidden on very small screens — but ideally they stay, even small.
