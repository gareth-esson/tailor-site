1 — Tailor Education

Tailor Education

Tailor Education is a community interest company focused on quality relationships and sex education. We deliver training, resources, and direct RSE provision to schools across the UK. Our work is evidence-based, age-appropriate, and grounded in what young people actually need to know.

As a CIC, all work goes back into improving RSE provision. We're not a charity, and we're not a traditional for-profit company — we're a professional practice dedicated to educational excellence. We believe in honest answers to the questions young people ask.

3 — The Okay to Ask project

Okay to Ask

What it is: a collection of real questions written by real young people on post-it notes during RSE lessons, answered honestly by Gareth.

Why it exists: because the questions young people actually ask are better than any curriculum document. They show what students are really thinking about — and they deserve straight answers.

The post-it question story: how the project started from a simple classroom activity and grew into a book and a website with 152 answered questions.

Annotations
→ Organisation first: Section 1 leads with Tailor Education, not an individual. The page establishes what the CIC does, its mission, and its values. The organisation is the brand and the primary focus.
→ Meet the team: Section 2 shows real people with photos — Gareth as founder plus team members. This approach emphasizes a professional practice, not a one-person operation. The tagline "Real people, real faces — this is the 'we' behind 'we deliver'" signals collaborative expertise.
→ Okay to Ask story: Section 3 is the emotional core of the page. The post-it origin story is what makes the project memorable and distinctive. This section should feel warm and personal — it's the "why" behind everything.
→ Brief by design: This page is context, not evidence. The depth lives on B3 (our approach) and B5 (services). The About page answers "what does this organisation do and who's behind it?" — the other pages answer "how do they work?"
→ Voice: We/our language throughout. The organisation speaks as a team, not Gareth as an individual. "We believe…", "We deliver…", "Our approach…" signals professional collaboration.

Design decisions flagged

Photo placement: Shown alongside the bio text (side-by-side on desktop). Could also be full-width above the text, or a smaller circular avatar. Side-by-side feels more editorial and professional; full-width feels more personal and bold.

Qualifications display: Could be inline in the bio text, or pulled out as a sidebar/callout. If Gareth has formal qualifications, DBS status, or professional memberships worth showing, they add credibility — but they shouldn't dominate.

Mobile: Photo stacks above the bio text. Okay to Ask section stays full-width. CTAs stack vertically.
