Legal / Statement page template
Shared template for text-heavy policy and statement pages. Currently two instances: B12 (Privacy policy) and B13 (Accessibility & inclusivity). Simple, readable structure — heading, optional table of contents, sectioned body text, contact block.

Annotations
→ Minimal template, maximum readability: These pages are text-first. No hero images, no cards, no complex layouts. The structure is: title → TOC → sections → contact. That's it. Readability is the only goal.
→ Plain English: Both instances should be written in Tailor Education's voice — warm, clear, human. The privacy policy should explain what data is collected and why, not hide behind legal boilerplate. The accessibility statement should feel like a commitment, not a checkbox.
→ "Last updated" date: Essential for legal pages. Shows the content is maintained. For privacy, it signals GDPR compliance. For accessibility, it shows the statement reflects the current site, not a launch-day draft that was never revisited.
→ TOC is optional: Short pages (2–3 sections) don't need a TOC — the content is visible in a single scroll. Longer pages (4+ sections) benefit from it. Both B12 and B13 have 5 sections, so both should use it.
→ No Simple Mode: These pages don't carry Simple Mode. They're not educational content — they're organisational statements. The language should still be clear and accessible, but there's no dual-version requirement.

Design decisions flagged

Max reading width: Body text should be constrained to a comfortable reading width (~65–75 characters per line). These are the most text-heavy pages on the site — long lines will kill readability.

Anchor link behaviour: TOC links should smooth-scroll to the target section and offset for the sticky header height. Standard behaviour, but worth noting for the developer.

Future instances: If Tailor Education adds a terms of service, cookie policy, or similar page, this same template handles it without any new wireframing. Just new content slotted into the same heading + body pattern.

Mobile: Everything stacks naturally. TOC may benefit from slightly smaller type or a collapsible "On this page" disclosure so it doesn't push the actual content too far down on small screens.
