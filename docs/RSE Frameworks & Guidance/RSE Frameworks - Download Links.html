Tailor Education — RSE Frameworks & Guidance

Direct download links for all key frameworks, statutory guidance, and international standards relevant to RSE content alignment. Click to download each document and save to this folder.

Note: All documents are publicly available from official sources. The DfE has two versions of the RSE guidance active right now — the current statutory guidance and the new guidance coming into force September 2026. Download both.
UK Statutory Guidance — DfE
Relationships Education, RSE and Health Education — Statutory Guidance (Current)
Statutory Current Department for Education
The primary statutory document. Maps out what must be taught at each key stage. Your activity library should map to this explicitly.
Download PDF GOV.UK page
Relationships Education, RSE and Health Education — New Guidance (September 2026)
Statutory Coming Sept 2026 Department for Education
The revised guidance coming into force September 2026. Start aligning your content to this now — schools will be preparing during the 2025–26 academic year.
Download PDF
Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE) 2025
Statutory Sept 2025 Department for Education
The safeguarding bible. Updated annually. Your activities touching on disclosure, abuse, exploitation, and FGM must reflect its current guidance. Schools will check this.
Download PDF GOV.UK page
FGM Mandatory Reporting — Procedural Information
Statutory Home Office / DfE
Teachers have a personal, non-transferable duty to report known FGM in under-18s to the police. Your FGM activities must explicitly reference this duty. This document is the source.
Download PDF GOV.UK page
Equality Act 2010 — Advice for Schools
Statutory Department for Education
Covers protected characteristics and how schools must comply. Directly relevant to RSE delivery around gender, religion, sexuality, and disability. Required reading for inclusive content design.
Download PDF GOV.UK page
Ofsted
Review of Sexual Abuse in Schools and Colleges (2021)
Guidance Ofsted — June 2021
The landmark report that called out weak RSHE as a contributing factor to normalised sexual harassment. Your activities on masculinity, consent, and online behaviour should directly respond to its findings. Schools are inspected against this.
Download PDF GOV.UK page
PSHE Association
PSHE Education Programme of Study (Key Stages 1–5)
Guidance PSHE Association — updated 2020
The professional standard for PSHE delivery. Goes beyond statutory guidance. The PSHE Association quality mark — which you should pursue — is assessed against this document. Your activity metadata should reference it by key stage.
Download PDF PSHE Association page
Sex Education Forum
Principles of Good RSE
Guidance Sex Education Forum (National Children's Bureau)
12 evidence-based principles for quality RSE. Schools are invited to commit to these publicly. Aligning your content to them and saying so gives you credibility with progressive, rights-based schools and local authorities.

Curriculum Design Tool for RSE
Guidance Sex Education Forum
Practical tool for structuring an RSE curriculum. Useful reference for how your activity library maps to a coherent programme.

International Frameworks
UNESCO International Technical Guidance on Sexuality Education (2018)
International UNESCO / UNFPA / WHO / UN Women / UNICEF / UNAIDS
The global standard for comprehensive sexuality education. Co-published by six UN agencies. Demonstrates that your approach is internationally evidence-based, not just UK-compliant. Useful for positioning Tailor as expert rather than just compliant.
Download PDF UNESCO page
WHO Standards for Sexuality Education in Europe
International WHO Regional Office for Europe / BZgA
Framework covering themes and learning objectives by age group. Referenced in professional RSE circles and useful for demonstrating your content is grounded in a holistic, developmental model rather than a purely compliance-driven one.
Download PDF
PSHE Association Quality Mark: Once your content is developed, apply for the PSHE Association quality mark at pshe-association.org.uk. This is the endorsement that will open the most school doors. The application process itself is a useful audit of your content against their standards.
