Annotations
→ Why not C5? The training page has a unique structure because of Section 3 (app connection). The three C5 service pages (delivery, drop-days, consultancy) don't have this — they're straightforward "what / who / included / enquire" pages. Training is the only service with a product tie-in.
→ App funnel: This is the highest-traffic service page because the Tailor app sends teachers here after they've used content. The page needs to convert visitors who already know the product into training enquiries.
→ Outcomes not modules: Section 2 deliberately frames learning as outcomes ("what changes for the teacher") rather than a curriculum of modules. Teachers don't care about module names — they care about whether this will help them.
E1 Enquiry form: Pre-tagged "CPD training". Could be rendered inline at the bottom of this page (replacing the CTA block with the actual form), or could link to /contact with the service pre-selected. Inline is higher-converting but adds page length.
→ No related blog posts or topics: Unlike C5, this page doesn't include related content sections. It's focused — training value prop → enquiry. Blog posts and topics are reachable from the nav. Adding them here would dilute the conversion funnel.

Design decisions flagged

Inline form vs link: Rendering E1 directly at the bottom of this page removes a click from the conversion path. But it adds length and may feel transactional. A compromise: show a condensed "express enquiry" (name, email, school, message) inline, with a link to the full form for those who want to specify key stage/timing.

App connection visual: Section 3 could be a simple text explanation or a more visual step-by-step flow (as wireframed). The flow diagram makes the relationship concrete — "training → unlocked content → ongoing teaching" — but needs to be simple enough to scan in 3 seconds.

Pricing: Not in the brief. If Gareth wants to show indicative pricing ("from £X"), it would sit in Section 4 (logistics) as a fifth card. If pricing is bespoke, the current approach works — everything drives to a conversation.

Mobile: The logistics grid (Section 4) stacks to a single column. The app flow steps (Section 3) stack vertically with downward arrows.
