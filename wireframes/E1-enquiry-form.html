Service Enquiry Form
The single conversion form for all services. Lives on /contact (B7) and/or inline on service pages (B4, C5). Seven fields — three required, four optional. The "service interested in" field pre-fills when arriving from a specific service page. Four states wireframed: empty (default), validation errors, submitting, and success.

Annotations
E1 Single form, multiple entry points: This form appears on /contact (B7), and is linked from every service CTA (A13) on B4, C5, and C4. The service select pre-fills via URL parameter (?service=training) or referrer detection.
D6 Four states: Empty (default), validation errors, submitting (loading), success confirmation. All four need designing. The pre-filled variant (State 1b) is a sub-state of empty.
→ Submit action: Sends to Gareth's email. No database storage on the content site. The form provider (Astro form action, Netlify Forms, Formspree, etc.) handles delivery. The privacy policy (B12) covers what happens to submitted data.
→ Spam protection: Needs a honeypot field or invisible reCAPTCHA. Not wireframed (invisible by design) but must be implemented. No visible CAPTCHA — it would undermine the lightweight feel.
→ Service options: 5 choices — Direct RSE delivery, CPD training, Drop day delivery, RSE consultancy, Other. "Other" is a catch-all for enquiries that don't fit a service line (e.g. "I want to buy the book in bulk").
→ Accessibility: All fields must have associated labels (not just placeholders). Error messages linked to fields via aria-describedby. Focus management on error — jump to first problem field. Keyboard navigable throughout.

Design decisions flagged

Inline vs dedicated page: The form could live inline at the bottom of B4 and C5 pages (higher converting — no extra click), or on a dedicated /contact page (cleaner pages, single form location). A hybrid is possible: short "express enquiry" inline (name, email, school, service) with a "full form" link for those who want to add detail.

Response timeframe: "2 working days" appears in both the intro text and the success message. This is a promise — it needs to be one Gareth can keep. If the real timeframe is longer, adjust both instances.

Optional field order: Key stage → timing → message. This mirrors the decision sequence: "who are the students?" → "when do you want this?" → "anything else?" Could reorder if Gareth finds timing is the most useful optional field.

Mobile: The form stacks naturally as a single column. No changes needed. The select dropdown uses the native mobile picker. The textarea should be tall enough to see what you're typing (minimum 3 lines visible).
