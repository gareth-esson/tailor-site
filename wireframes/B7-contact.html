Annotations
→ E1 lives here: The enquiry form is embedded inline as section 1 — this is where the form lives. Every "Get in touch" CTA across the site (A12, A13, service pages, services hub) links to /contact, optionally with a ?service= parameter to pre-select the service dropdown.
→ Form first, details second: The form is the primary conversion mechanism and comes first. Direct contact details are secondary — for people who already know what they want and would rather email directly than fill in a form.
→ Safeguarding placement: The safeguarding note sits at the bottom — visible but not dominant. It's there for responsibility, not prominence. It prevents someone using the enquiry form for a safeguarding concern and being disappointed by a slow response.
→ No map or address: Tailor Education is a CIC that delivers in schools, not a venue. A postal/office address and map would be misleading. Contact details are people-based (email, phone, social), not location-based.

Design decisions flagged

Form prominence: The form should feel like the main event — large, clear, not buried. The intro copy above it should be warm and low-pressure ("we'd love to hear from you" not "submit your enquiry").

Direct contact vs. form: These are complementary, not competing. The "Or reach out directly" heading makes the relationship clear — the form is preferred, but direct contact is always available.

Phone number: Gareth to decide whether a phone number is public. If not, email alone is fine — the section still works.

Success state: After form submission, the page should show the E1 success state (wireframed in E1-enquiry-form.html) in place of the form. Direct contact and social links remain visible below.

Mobile: Everything stacks naturally. The form fields are already full-width in E1. Social icons and contact details remain tappable.
