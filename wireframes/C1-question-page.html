Annotations
A9 Crisis support: Above everything. Only on safeguarding questions. Visually distinct — must be noticeable without being alarming. Not dismissable.
D1 Age flag: Between post-it and answer. Not a gate — just an honest signpost. "Continue to answer" reveals content. Answer is always accessible.
A6 Simple Mode toggle: Near the answer, before text begins. Should stay accessible as reader scrolls (sticky/fixed — design decision). Persists across site via localStorage. Must check localStorage before first paint — no flash of wrong content.
A7 Glossary tooltips: Inline in the answer text. First occurrence only per page. Desktop: hover. Mobile: tap. Shows Short Definition (standard) or Simple Definition (simple mode). "Read more →" links to /glossary/{slug}.
A8 Signposting: Always present. Contextual per question. Comes before the end-of-answer panel (A17). Not dismissable. Services from: Childline, Brook, The Mix, NSPCC, Women's Aid, NHS Sexual Health, Galop.
A17 End-of-answer panel: Three panels — book / teach this topic / bring to school. Serves all three audiences. Helpful tone, not an ad strip. Positioned after the signposting block (A8). Mobile: stack vertically. Book panel needs placeholder state until sales URL is finalised.
A15 Related questions: 3–5 cards with post-it thumbnails. Links to other question pages. Draws the reader deeper into the Okay to Ask content.
A16 Topic tags: Links to parent landing page(s). Possibly colour-coded by category.
D7 Dual-answer variant: ~10–15% of questions. Two answers stacked, both visible. Each has its own Simple variant. Labels TBD by Gareth ("For younger readers" / "For older readers" or similar).

Design decisions flagged

Simple Mode toggle placement: Should it be sticky/fixed as the reader scrolls, or positioned at the top of the answer only? If the answer is long, the reader may want to switch mid-read. Sticky is better for usability but adds visual complexity.

Simple Mode transition (D5): When toggled, the content swaps between Standard and Simple. Both versions exist in the HTML — the toggle shows/hides. Should the swap be instant, fade, or slide? Must check localStorage before first paint to render the correct version immediately — no flash.

Crisis support vs signposting: A9 (crisis) appears above the answer on safeguarding questions. A8 (signposting) appears below the answer on all questions. They may show the same service (e.g. Childline) — that's intentional. The crisis block is urgent; the signposting block is a reference.

Dual-answer framing: Gareth to decide the labels — "For younger readers" / "For older readers", or "The short answer" / "The full answer", or something else. The framing matters — it should feel like two valid perspectives, not a dumbed-down version and a real one.

Mobile: All sections stack naturally. The end-of-answer panel (A17) stacks to three full-width cards. Related questions may become a horizontal scroll or a 2-column grid.
