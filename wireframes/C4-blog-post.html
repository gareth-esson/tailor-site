Annotations
A7 Glossary tooltips: Active throughout the body content. Same behaviour as C1 and C2 — first occurrence only, hover on desktop, tap on mobile.
A13 CTA block: Two variants. When Service Link is set: "Want help with [service]? Talk to us" → enquiry form pre-tagged. When Service Link = "none": "Explore lessons on [topic]" → app library. The variant is driven by a property in the Blog DB.
A15 Related posts: 2–3 related blog posts, shown as cards. Matching is by topic tag or manual curation. Uses the blog variant of the A15 card component.
A16 Topic tags: Positioned near the top (Section 3) so the reader sees topic context before reading. Links to the relevant C3 landing page(s).
→ No Simple Mode: Blog posts don't have Standard/Simple variants. They're written for a professional audience (teachers, school leaders, parents). Simple Mode only applies to C1 (questions) and C2 (glossary).
→ Inline CTAs: Gareth may write natural links into the body text pointing to service pages, the app, or training. These are hand-authored, not templated — the template only provides the bottom CTA (A13).

Design decisions flagged

Audience label placement: The label ("For teachers") appears in the meta line. It could also appear more prominently — as a banner or badge at the top. The current placement is subtle; Gareth may want it louder if the blog serves multiple distinct audiences.

Topic tags position: Shown here near the top (Section 3) for context. Could alternatively sit at the bottom alongside related posts. Top placement helps teachers immediately see whether the post is relevant to their needs.

Related posts logic: Auto-matched by topic tag, or manually curated per post? Auto-matching is easier to maintain but may surface less relevant results. A "related posts" relation field in Notion gives Gareth manual control.

Reading time: Not in the brief, but could be a useful addition to the meta line. "5 min read" helps busy teachers decide whether to commit.
